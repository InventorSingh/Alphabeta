Project Alphabeta: Pilot Workshops

Short and focused workshops to help 11th and 12th graders explore career paths—before we invest in a larger facility.

Objective

  • Validate student interest in online career workshops.
  • Gather feedback for refining our approach.
  • Build brand recognition before the physical center launch.

Target Audience

  • 11th & 12th graders seeking career guidance in:
    • Technology (Software, AI, Data Science)
    • Government Jobs (Banking, UPSC basics, etc.)
    • Private/Corporate Sectors (Business, Finance, HR)
  • Parents and counselors who support student decisions.

Workshop Topics

  1. Tech & Emerging Roles: Overview of software dev, data science, and future tech.
  2. Government Exam Basics: Eligibility, exam patterns, and study tips.
  3. Private Sector Pathways: Corporate roles, soft skills, and internships.

Delivery & Schedule

  • Format: Live Zoom/Meet sessions (1–2 hours each)
  • Frequency: One workshop per week, total of 3 sessions
  • Timeline:
    • Prep & Promotion: 2 weeks
    • Workshops: 3 weeks (1 per week)
    • Feedback & Analysis: 1 week

Key Takeaways

  • Establish Alphabeta credibility through useful workshops.
  • Collect real-time feedback from students to refine content.
  • Build an interested community for future, larger-scale programs.

Next Steps

  1. Finalize workshop dates and speaker lineup.
  2. Launch brief online promotion (flyers, social media, school outreach).
  3. Conduct workshops, gather surveys & feedback.
  4. Analyze outcomes & plan for bigger programs/facility launch.
